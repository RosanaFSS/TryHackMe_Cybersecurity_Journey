<p>True or False: An SQL injection vulnerability presents higher risk on external-facing applications.<br>
✅  &nbsp;&nbsp; True.<br>
⬛  &nbsp;&nbsp; False<br>
❯❯❯❯ &nbsp;&nbsp; External-facing applications have greater threat exposure, increasing likelihood of exploitation and overall risk.</p>

<h2></h2>
<p>How many stages comprise the Cyber Kill Chain framework introduced by Lockheed Martin?<br>  
⬛ &nbsp;&nbsp; 5<br>
⬛ &nbsp;&nbsp; 6<br>
✅ &nbsp;&nbsp; 7<br>
⬛ &nbsp;&nbsp; 9<br>
⬛ &nbsp;&nbsp; 8<br>
❯❯❯❯ &nbsp;&nbsp; The Cyber Kill Chain consists of 7 stages from reconnaissance to actions on objectives.</p>

<h2></h2>
<p>True or False: IDOR vulnerabilities allow access to resources belonging to other users.<br>
✅  &nbsp;&nbsp; True.<br>
⬛  &nbsp;&nbsp; False<br>
❯❯❯❯ &nbsp;&nbsp; IDOR (Insecure Direct Object Reference) vulnerabilities occur when applications<br>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; fail to verify user authotization for accessing specific resources.</p>

<h2></h2>
<p>True or False: Penetration testers operate with explicit written consent from system owners.<br>  
✅  &nbsp;&nbsp; True.<br>
⬛  &nbsp;&nbsp; False<br>
❯❯❯❯ &nbsp;&nbsp; Authorization through written consent is a core requirement that separates penetration testing from malicious hacking.</p>

<h2></h2>
<p>True or False: The Cyber Kill Chain consists of seven distinct phases.<br>
✅  &nbsp;&nbsp; True.<br>
⬛  &nbsp;&nbsp; False<br>
❯❯❯❯ &nbsp;&nbsp; The Cyber Kill Chain framework divides attacks into seven stages: <br>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Reconnaissance, Weaponisation, Delivery, Exploitation, Installation, Command & Control, and Actions on Objectives.</p>

<h2></h2>
<p>Match each Cyber Kill Chain phase with its primary purpose:<br>
<strong>Reconnaissance</strong> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; : &nbsp;&nbsp; Gathering target informations<br>
<strong>Installation</strong> &nbsp;&nbsp&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; : &nbsp;&nbsp; Maintaining persistent access<br>
<strong>Delivery</strong> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; : &nbsp;&nbsp; Transmitting exploit code<br>
<strong>Weaponisation</strong> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; : &nbsp;&nbsp; Creating malicious payloads</p>
